    CHARLES'S LAW

    This law says that in an area where pressure is kept constant, volume will increase as the temperature increases, and vice versa The pressure is constant, volume and temperature are directly proportional with one another.

    Example: Heating up a balloon makes it expand.

    Formula: V1/T1=V2/T2

    Sample Problem: A gas occupies 900.0 mL at a temperature of 27.0℃. What is the volume at 132.0℃?

    Step 1 - Find the given
    V1 - 900.0 mL
    T1 - 27.0℃
    V2 - ?
    T2 - 132.0℃

    Step 2 - Before solving for the final volume, we need to change celsius into kelvin by adding 273
    T1 - 27.0℃ + 273 = 300K
    T2 - 132.0℃ + 273 = 405K

    Step 3 - Now that the temperature values are in kelvin, we can substitute the values using the formula
    V1/T1=V2/T2 → 900.0 mL / 300K = V2 / 405K

    Step 4 - Cross multiply the given values
    900.0 mL / 300K = V2 / 405K → (900.0 mL)(405K) = (V2)(300K)

    Step 5 - Multiply the values at the left side of the equal sign. (We don’t need to multiply the values at the right since we are missing a value)
    (900.0 mL)(405K) = (V2)(300K) → 364,500 = (V2)(300K)

    Step 6 - In order to solve for the missing value, divide each side by 300K and cross out the 300K at the right side so that all that will be left is V2
    364,500 = (V2)(300K) → 364,500 / 300K= (V2)(300K) / 300K

    Step 7 - Divide the values at the left
    364,500 / 300K= (V2)(300K) / 300K → 1215 mL = V2

    Step 8 - Write the final answer
    Final Answer: At 132.0℃, the volume of the gas is 1215 mL.
