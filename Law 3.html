    GAY LUSSAC'S LAW

    This law says that in an area where pressure is kept constant, volume will increase as the temperature increases, and vice versa The pressure is constant, volume and temperature are directly proportional with one another.

    Example: heating up a sealed can may lead to it exploding due to it having an increase in its temperature

    Formula: P1/T1=P2/T2

    Sample Problem: The pressure of a gas in a cylinder when it is heated to a temperature of 250K is 1.5 atm. What was the initial temperature of the gas if its initial pressure was 1 atm?

    Step 1 - Find the Given
    P1 - 1 atm
    T1 - ?
    P2 - 1.5 atm
    T2 - 250K

    Step 2 - Substitute all the given values in the formula
    P1 / T1 = P2 / T2
    1 atm / T1 = 1.5 atm / 250 K

    Step 3 - Solve for the missing variable

    *cross multiply
    1 atm / T1 = 1.5 atm / 250 K
    250 atm·K = 1.5 atm x T1

    *isolate T1 by dividing both sides by 1.5 atm
    250 atm·K / 1.5 atm = 1.5 atm x T1 / 1.5 atm
    250 atm·k / 1.5 atm = T1

    *and finally, simply divide
    250 atm.k / 1.5 atm = T1
    166.67 K = T1

    Step 4 - Write the final answer
    Final Answer: The initial temperature of the gas is 166.67K.
