    BOYLE'S LAW

    Temperature is constant, volume and pressure are inversely proportional. This law says that in an area where temperature is kept constant, pressure will increase as the volume decreases, and vice versa.

    Example: Compressing gas decreases its volume and increases its pressure.

    Formula: P1 V1= P2 V2

    Sample Problem: A container of oxygen has a volume of 30.0 mL and a pressure of 4.00 atm. If the pressure of the oxygen gas is reduced to 2.00 atm and the pressure is kept constant, what is the new volume of the oxygen gas?

    Step 1 - Find the given
    P1 = 4.00 atm
    V1 = 30.0 mL
    P2 = 2.00 atm
    V2 = ?

    Step 2 - Substitute the given values using the formula
    P1 V1= P2 V2 → (4.00 atm)(30.0 mL) = (2.00 atm)(V2)

    Step 3 - Multiply the values at the left side of the equal sign. (We don’t need to multiply the values at the right since we are missing a value)
    (4.00 atm)(30.0 mL) = (2.00 atm)(V2) → 120 = (2.00 atm)(V2)

    Step 4 - In order to solve for the missing value, divide each side by 2.00 and cross out the 2.00 at the right side so that all that will be left is V2.
    120 = (2.00 atm)(V2) → 120 / 2.00 = (2.00 atm)(V2) / 2.00

    Step 5 - Divide the values at the left
    120 / 2.00 = (2.00 atm)(V2) / 2.00 → 60 mL = V2

    Step 6 - Write the final answer
    Final answer: The new volume of the oxygen gas is 60 mL.
