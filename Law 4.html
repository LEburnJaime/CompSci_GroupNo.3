    AVOGADRO'S LAW

    The pressure and temperature are kept constant, the volume of a gas is directly proportional to the number of moles of gas.

    Formula: V1/N1=V2/N2

    Sample Problem: A flexible container at an initial volume of 5.00 L contains 6.500 mol of has. More gas is added to the flexible container until it reaches a final volume of 18.30 L. The pressure and the temperature of the gas remain constant. How many moles were added to the container?

    Step 1 - Find the Given
    V1 = 5.00 L
    N1 = 6.500 mol
    V2 = 18.30 L
    N2 = ?

    Step 2 - Substitute all the given values in the formula
    V1 / N1 = V2 / N2
    5.00 L / 6.500 mol = 18.30 L / N2

    Step 3 - Solve for the missing variable

    *cross multiply
    5.00 L / 6.500 mol = 18.30 L / N2
    5.00 L x N2 = 118.95 L路mol

    *isolate N2 by dividing both sides by 5.00L
    5.00 L x N2 / 5.00L = 118.95 L路mol / 5.00 L
    118.95 L路mol / 5.00 L = N2

    *and finally, simply divide
    118.95 L路mol / 5.00 L = N2
    23.79 mol = N2

    Step 4 - To find how many moles were added, we subtract 6.500 mol from 23.79 (the total number of moles)
    23.79 - 6.500 mol = 17.29 mol

    Step 5 - Write the final answer
    Final Answer: There are 17.29 moles added to the container.
